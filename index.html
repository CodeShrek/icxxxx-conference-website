About the Organizers & Partners

This conference marks a significant milestone in our journey toward fostering global academic collaboration and technological advancement in AI, Utilities, and Renewables.

Faculty of Technology

As the premier engineering and technology faculty at DU, we are dedicated to building the future through cutting-edge research, innovative pedagogy, and a forward-looking approach to computational sciences.

University of Delhi

Established in 1922, the University of Delhi is recognized globally for its academic excellence. It serves as the historic and intellectual foundation for this international gathering.

Springer Nature

We are proud to collaborate with Springer. Accepted papers will be published in Springer Lecture Notes in Electrical Engineering (LNEE), which is Scopus Indexed.

Call for Papers

We invite original research contributions (8-15 pages, Springer Format) aligned with the conference themes. Accepted papers will be published in Springer Lecture Notes in Electrical Engineering (LNEE) Scopus Indexed.

Track 1: AI in Smart Utilities and Infrastructure

  • AI-driven demand forecasting and load management
  • Intelligent grid optimization and fault prediction
  • Smart metering and energy usage analytics
  • AI in water, waste, and gas management systems
  • Predictive analytics for utility maintenance

Track 2: AI for Renewable Energy Systems

  • Solar and wind energy forecasting using AI
  • Energy storage optimization
  • Integration of renewables into smart grids
  • AI models for microgrid control
  • Real-time performance monitoring of renewable assets

Track 3: Industrial Automation and Robotics

  • AI in manufacturing process optimization
  • Machine vision for quality inspection
  • Predictive maintenance using deep learning
  • Autonomous robots in industrial environments
  • Human-robot collaboration in smart factories

Track 4: IoT and Edge AI for Energy & Automation

  • Intelligent sensor networks for energy efficiency
  • Edge computing for real-time control
  • IoT-based predictive diagnostics
  • Digital twins for utility and automation systems
  • Distributed AI in cyber-physical systems

Track 5: Data Science and Optimization in Sustainable Systems

  • AI/ML for energy efficiency and carbon footprint reduction
  • Big Data and cloud analytics in smart energy systems
  • Optimization algorithms for smart resource allocation
  • Energy consumption modeling using neural networks
  • Reinforcement learning for dynamic control

Track 6: Cybersecurity and Ethics in AI-driven Systems

  • Threat detection and anomaly identification in utility networks
  • Privacy-preserving AI in infrastructure
  • Secure automation protocols and AI compliance
  • Ethical implications of AI in public utilities
  • Trustworthy AI and explainability in critical systems

Track 7: Emerging applications of AI

  • Green Technologies based on SDGs
  • Industry 5.0
  • Quantum computing
